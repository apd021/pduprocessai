Business Translation: Cybersecurity Governance

Business Value

Establish organizational capability to systematically manage cybersecurity risks, ensuring compliance with regulations and protecting brand reputation.

Risk Reduction

  • Regulatory Compliance: Meet UNECE WP.29 R155 requirements, avoiding certification delays and market access issues
  • Reputation Protection: Prevent cybersecurity incidents that could damage brand value and customer trust
  • Liability Mitigation: Demonstrate due diligence in cybersecurity management, reducing legal exposure
  • Supply Chain Confidence: Enable customers to trust your cybersecurity capabilities

Stakeholder Impact

  • Customers (OEMs): Increased confidence in supplier cybersecurity maturity
  • Regulators: Demonstrated compliance with automotive cybersecurity regulations
  • Investors: Reduced cybersecurity risk exposure, protecting company valuation
  • Management: Clear accountability and visibility into cybersecurity posture
  • Engineering Teams: Clear processes and tools, reducing ambiguity and rework

Compliance Benefits

  • UNECE WP.29 R155 type approval requirements
  • ISO/SAE 21434 certification readiness
  • Customer cybersecurity assessment requirements
  • Industry best practices alignment

Cost Considerations

  • Initial Investment: Policy development, process documentation, tool setup (3-6 months)
  • Ongoing Costs: Training, audits, tool maintenance (~2-5% of engineering budget)
  • ROI: Reduced rework, faster certification, avoided incident costs
  • Cost of Non-Compliance: Certification delays, market access restrictions, potential recalls

Market Positioning

  • Differentiate as cybersecurity-mature supplier
  • Qualify for high-security vehicle programs
  • Support premium pricing for secure products
  • Enable entry into regulated markets
